Welcome

This site contains computational activities for first-year university mathematics. The activities use Python notebooks to support exploration, visualization, and conceptual understanding.

🌟 Project Inspiration

This project was inspired by the Computational Teaching and Learning with Mathematics Workshop held at the Banff International Research Station (BIRS) in 2025.

Participation in this workshop highlighted the potential of computational approaches to support mathematical exploration, visualization, and conceptual understanding. The activities on this site were developed as a way to bring some of these ideas into first-year university mathematics courses.

🙏 Acknowledgements

This project has also been influenced by the excellent work of Patrick Walls , whose open educational resources demonstrate the power of Python, Jupyter notebooks, and computation for learning undergraduate mathematics.

His materials helped shape many of the ideas behind using computation as a tool for exploration, visualization, and mathematical discovery.
